# Numerical verification

Both scripts are self-contained pure-Python (plus `sympy` for polynomial gcd/division
in `verify_dual_lemmas.py`) and were written independently of the proof text, from
scratch, precisely because no computer algebra system was available — so they do not
share code or conventions with any CAS the proof authors might have used.

Everything is built on **Macaulay inverse systems with the contraction action**
(divided powers), which is valid over every field — in particular the checks are
meaningful in small positive characteristic, where naive differentiation-based
inverse systems silently fail.

## `verify_dual_lemmas.py`

Constructs Artinian level type-2 quotients of $k[x_1,x_2,x_3]$ as $A = R/\mathrm{Ann}(W)$
for random, sparse, and specially engineered 2-dimensional spaces $W$ of dual forms of
degree $e$ (every type-2 level algebra arises this way), over
$\mathbb{F}_2, \mathbb{F}_3, \mathbb{F}_5, \mathbb{Q}$, socle degrees $3 \le e \le 7$.
For each example it checks:

- **[L1a]** the socle profile is $(0,\ldots,0,2)$ — equivalent, via the Koszul identity
  $\mathrm{Tor}_3(A,k)_j \cong \mathrm{Soc}(A)_{j-3}$, to the claim that the
  last module in the minimal free resolution is $R(-e-3)^2$;
- **[L1b]** the reindexed Matlis dual $M$ is generated by exactly two elements, both in
  degree 0;
- **[L8]** wherever the minimal relations of degree $< d$ span a rank-one line over
  $\mathrm{Frac}(R)$: an equal-degree primitive $v \in R^2$ exists (recovered by
  gcd), and every low relation is an exact polynomial multiple of it — including
  engineered cases with a non-principal multiplier ideal $J$, where $v$ must be
  extracted by cancelling a genuine common factor;
- **[LC]** the Hilbert function is log-concave (end-to-end check of the theorem).

Result as committed: **248/248 examples pass; 80 rank-one configurations exercised;
zero failures.**

## `stress_stanley.py`

Directly stress-tests the one substantive external input of the proof
(Stanley's theorem, characteristic-free by Zanello's proof): generates 810 random and
sparse Artinian Gorenstein quotients of $k[x_1,x_2,x_3]$ over
$\mathbb{F}_2, \mathbb{F}_3, \mathbb{F}_5$ with socle degrees $2 \le E \le 10$ and checks
that every $h$-vector is symmetric, nondecreasing through $\lfloor E/2 \rfloor$, and a
full SI-sequence (first difference of the first half satisfies the Macaulay growth
bound, implemented via binomial expansions).

Result as committed: **810/810 pass; zero failures.**

## Caveats

Numerical checks over finite ranges prove nothing about the general statement; they
are here to catch the failure modes that historically kill candidate proofs in this
area (characteristic-dependence, off-by-one index errors, degenerate configurations),
and they found none in the final argument.
