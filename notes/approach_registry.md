## Global audit traps

- Ordinary derivatives fail as inverse systems in small characteristic; use divided powers or contraction.
- Macaulay growth alone does not encode levelness.
- A sum/intersection of Gorenstein objects may lose log-concavity.
- The last nontrivial index is `e-1`, not `e-2`: `2 h[e-2] <= h[e-1]^2`.
- No appeal to global unimodality, flawlessness, differentiability, WLP, or Interval Conjecture without a proof.

## Proven boundary lemmas

- `i=1` is immediate from `h_0=1,h_1=3,h_2<=6`.
- `i=2`: the only Macaulay-possible failure forces `(h_2,h_3)=(3,4)`; then the three-dimensional quadratic ideal piece has shadow dimension at most six, hence a common linear factor, producing a forbidden degree-one socle element.
- `i=e-1`: a characteristic-free first-prolongation bound for a concise subspace of `k^2 tensor (k^3)^*` gives `2 h[e-2] <= h[e-1]^2`.

## Candidate global mechanism (2026-07-10)

- For reversed `g`, the dual resolution is `0 -> R(-e-3) -> F2 -> F1 -> R^2 -> M`.
- The unique fraction-field relation among all `F2` columns has every coordinate nonzero, so every proper column subset is independent.
- At a putative failed triple, cumulative degree-strand ranks leave only a rank-one family of low relations.
- Factoring those relations as `vJ`, the image of `v` generates a cyclic submodule whose quotient by its annihilator is codimension-at-most-three Gorenstein.  The global degree bound locates the needed difference in its first half, where Stanley's characteristic-free SI theorem makes it nonnegative.
- The resulting LC margin is `x(x+1)-b >= d(d+1)-b >= 0`.
